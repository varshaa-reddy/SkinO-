Essentials that should be present in your cleanser

A cleanser for dry skin should be gentle, hydrating, and non-stripping, helping to cleanse without removing essential moisture. It should contain hydrating agents like Hyaluronic Acid, Glycerin, Aloe Vera, and Honey to retain moisture, nourishing ingredients such as Shea Butter, Squalane, and Ceramides to strengthen the skin barrier, and soothing components like Colloidal Oatmeal, Chamomile Extract, and Cucumber Extract to reduce irritation. A good cleanser for dry skin should be fragrance-free, sulfate-free, and alcohol-free, ensuring a soft, smooth, and well-hydrated complexion without tightness or discomfort.

Essentials that should be present in your Toner

A toner for dry skin should be deeply hydrating, soothing, and barrier-strengthening to replenish moisture and maintain skin health. It should include hydrating agents like Hyaluronic Acid, Glycerin, Aloe Vera, and Cucumber Extract to keep the skin plump and moisturized, soothing ingredients such as Rose Water, Chamomile Extract, and Colloidal Oatmeal to calm irritation, and nourishing components like Squalane, Ceramides, and Panthenol (Vitamin B5) to repair and strengthen the skin barrier. A good toner for dry skin should be alcohol-free, fragrance-free, and non-astringent, ensuring that it refreshes the skin without causing dryness or irritation.

Essentials that should be present in your serum

A serum for dry skin should be intensely hydrating, nourishing, and barrier-repairing to restore moisture and improve skin health. It should contain deep hydrators like Hyaluronic Acid, Glycerin, and Polyglutamic Acid to retain moisture, nourishing oils such as Squalane, Jojoba Oil, and Marula Oil to soften and replenish the skin, and barrier-strengthening ingredients like Ceramides, Peptides, and Panthenol (Vitamin B5) to repair and protect. Additionally, soothing agents like Aloe Vera, Centella Asiatica, and Colloidal Oatmeal help reduce irritation, while antioxidants such as Vitamin E and Niacinamide provide extra protection against environmental damage. A good serum for dry skin should have a lightweight yet deeply moisturizing formula that absorbs easily without leaving a greasy residue.

Essentials that should be present in your moisturizer

A moisturizer for dry skin should be deeply hydrating, nourishing, and barrier-repairing. It should include Hyaluronic Acid, Glycerin, and Urea for hydration, Shea Butter, Squalane, and Cocoa Butter for nourishment, and Ceramides, Peptides, and Panthenol to strengthen the skin barrier. Occlusives like Petrolatum and Dimethicone help lock in moisture, while Aloe Vera, Oat Extract, and Centella Asiatica soothe irritation. Antioxidants like Vitamin E and Green Tea Extract protect against damage. A rich, creamy texture ensures long-lasting hydration without greasiness.

Essentials that should be present in your sunscreen

A sunscreen for dry skin should provide broad-spectrum SPF 30+ protection while keeping the skin hydrated and nourished. It should include Zinc Oxide or Titanium Dioxide for mineral protection or Avobenzone and Tinosorb for chemical protection. Hydrating ingredients like Hyaluronic Acid, Glycerin, and Squalane help retain moisture, while Ceramides and Shea Butter strengthen the skin barrier. Antioxidants like Vitamin E and Green Tea Extract protect against environmental damage. A creamy, non-greasy, fragrance-free formula ensures long-lasting moisture and smooth application.
