Essentials that should be used for normal skin

To fade dark spots on normal skin, use Vitamin C, a powerful antioxidant that brightens the skin and reduces hyperpigmentation. Niacinamide helps even out skin tone while improving the skin barrier. Alpha Arbutin works as a natural skin-lightening agent, reducing melanin production. Licorice Extract soothes the skin and helps fade dark spots over time. Use a Vitamin C serum in the morning, followed by sunscreen to prevent further pigmentation, and a niacinamide or alpha arbutin serum at night for best results.

Essentials that should be used for Dry skin

Dark spots on dry skin require brightening ingredients that also provide hydration. Hyaluronic Acid ensures deep hydration while supporting skin repair. Vitamin C brightens the complexion and boosts collagen production. Shea Butter deeply nourishes and prevents further skin damage. Lactic Acid, a mild exfoliant, helps remove dead skin cells and promote cell turnover without drying out the skin. Use a creamy Vitamin C serum in the morning and a hydrating exfoliating treatment with lactic acid at night to gradually fade spots.

Essentials that should be used for oily skin

Oily skin benefits from Niacinamide, which controls excess oil and fades hyperpigmentation while strengthening the skin barrier. Salicylic Acid exfoliates the skin and unclogs pores, reducing post-acne dark spots. Kojic Acid is a potent skin-brightening agent that blocks melanin production. Tea Tree Oil helps prevent future breakouts while reducing inflammation and redness. Use an oil-free Vitamin C or niacinamide serum in the morning and a lightweight kojic acid treatment at night to target dark spots.

Essentials that should be used for combination skin

Dark spot treatment for combination skin should focus on balancing hydration while treating pigmentation. Vitamin C brightens the skin and evens out tone, while Mandelic Acid provides gentle exfoliation for both dry and oily areas. Niacinamide controls oil in the T-zone while strengthening the skin barrier. Licorice Extract soothes dry patches and reduces dark spots. Use a lightweight Vitamin C serum in the morning, and apply a gentle exfoliant like mandelic acid 2-3 times a week at night for best results.

Essentials that should be used for sensitive skin

Dark spots on sensitive skin require gentle but effective ingredients. Azelaic Acid helps reduce pigmentation while calming redness and irritation. Centella Asiatica soothes the skin and supports healing. Oat Extract hydrates and protects the skin barrier while reducing inflammation. Vitamin E is an antioxidant that promotes skin healing and prevents further dark spots. Use an azelaic acid serum at night and a soothing Vitamin E cream in the morning, ensuring daily SPF use to prevent further pigmentation.
