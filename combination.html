Essentials that should be present in your cleanser

A cleanser for combination skin should effectively balance oil production while maintaining hydration. It should include gentle surfactants like Cocamidopropyl Betaine to cleanse without stripping moisture, hydrating agents like Glycerin and Hyaluronic Acid to retain hydration in dry areas, and oil-controlling ingredients like Niacinamide, Green Tea Extract, or Salicylic Acid to regulate excess sebum in oily zones. Additionally, antioxidants such as Vitamin C and Aloe Vera soothe and protect the skin, while avoiding harsh sulfates, alcohol, and artificial fragrances helps maintain the skinâ€™s natural barrier. A gel-based or mild foaming cleanser is ideal for keeping the skin fresh, balanced, and healthy.

Essentials that should be present in your Toner

A toner for combination skin should balance oil and hydration while keeping the skin refreshed and soothed. It should contain hydrating ingredients like Hyaluronic Acid, Aloe Vera, and Glycerin to nourish dry areas, while oil-controlling agents like Niacinamide, Green Tea Extract, and Witch Hazel help regulate excess sebum in oily zones. Gentle exfoliants such as Lactic Acid or PHA (Polyhydroxy Acid) can help unclog pores and smooth the skin without over-drying. Additionally, antioxidants like Vitamin C and Chamomile Extract protect the skin from environmental stress. An alcohol-free formula is best to prevent irritation and maintain skin balance.

Essentials that should be present in your serum

A serum for combination skin should provide balanced hydration, oil control, and nourishment. It should contain Hyaluronic Acid and Glycerin to hydrate dry areas without making the skin greasy, while Niacinamide and Zinc help regulate excess oil production in oily zones. Antioxidants like Vitamin C and Green Tea Extract protect against environmental stressors and brighten the complexion. Lightweight oils like Squalane provide nourishment without clogging pores, and gentle exfoliants like Lactic Acid or PHA help keep the skin smooth and even. A non-greasy, fast-absorbing formula ensures that both dry and oily areas stay balanced and healthy.

Essentials that should be present in your moisturizer

A moisturizer for combination skin should provide balanced hydration, nourishment, and oil control. It should contain Hyaluronic Acid and Glycerin to hydrate dry areas while keeping the skin lightweight. Niacinamide and Green Tea Extract help regulate excess oil and soothe irritation. Squalane and Jojoba Oil offer nourishment without clogging pores, while Ceramides and Peptides strengthen the skin barrier. A lightweight, non-comedogenic, and fast-absorbing formula ensures hydration without making oily areas greasy, keeping the skin smooth, healthy, and well-balanced.

Essentials that should be present in your sunscreen

A sunscreen for combination skin should provide broad-spectrum SPF 30+ or higher protection while maintaining a balanced finish. It should contain Zinc Oxide or Titanium Dioxide for mineral protection or Avobenzone and Tinosorb for chemical protection. Hyaluronic Acid and Glycerin help hydrate dry areas, while Niacinamide and Green Tea Extract control excess oil and soothe the skin. A lightweight, non-greasy, and mattifying yet hydrating formula ensures that both dry and oily areas stay comfortable, making it ideal for daily wear.
